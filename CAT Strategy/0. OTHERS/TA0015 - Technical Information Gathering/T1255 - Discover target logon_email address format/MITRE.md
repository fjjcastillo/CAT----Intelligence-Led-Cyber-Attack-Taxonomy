 <h1>
  Discover target logon/email address format
 </h1>
 <div class="row">
  <div class="col-md-8 description-body">
   <p>
    Email addresses, logon credentials, and other forms of online identification typically share a common format. This makes guessing other credentials within the same domain easier. For example if a known email address is first.last@company.com it is likely that others in the company will have an email in the same format.

 <h2 class="pt-3" id="detectable">
  Detection
 </h2>
 <b>
  Detectable by Common Defenses (Yes/No/Partial):
 </b>
 No
 <br/>
 <br/>
 <b>
  Explanation:
 </b>
 Easily determined and not intended to be protected information. Publicly collected and shared repositories of email addresses exist.
 <br/>
 <br/>
 <h2 class="pt-3" id="difficulty">
  Difficulty for the Adversary
 </h2>
 <b>
  Easy for the Adversary (Yes/No):
 </b>
 Yes
 <br/>
 <br/>
 <b>
  Explanation:
 </b>
 Scraping of known email addresses from the target will likely reveal the target standard for address/username format.  This information is easily discoverable.
